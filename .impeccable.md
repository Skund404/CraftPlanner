# CraftPlanner Design System

## Design Context

### Users
CraftPlanner serves two overlapping audiences:
- **Hobbyist makers**: Cosplayers, leatherworkers, woodworkers, sewists. Creative but not tech-savvy. Use the app at a workbench or desk between making sessions. Want to track progress, costs, and materials without friction.
- **Semi-professional creators**: Commission-based makers, Etsy sellers, small studio operators. Need cost tracking, time logging, and structured project management. Templates and custom fields give them the depth they need.

Both audiences share one trait: they are **makers first, software users second**. The interface must never make them feel like they're operating enterprise software. Every interaction should feel as natural as flipping through a project notebook.

### Brand Personality
**Crafted. Warm. Capable.**

- **Crafted**: The interface itself should feel handmade — like a well-loved leather notebook or a carefully organized workshop. Attention to detail in typography, spacing, and micro-interactions signals that someone cared about every pixel.
- **Warm**: Earth tones, tactile textures, generous whitespace. The app should feel like sitting at a clean workbench with good light, not staring at a terminal.
- **Capable**: Despite the warmth, the underlying system is powerful. Templates, custom fields, unit conversion, linked primitives — the depth is there when you need it, hidden when you don't.

Emotional target: the quiet satisfaction of a well-organized workspace.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone**: Warm, organic, tactile. Think analog tools rendered digitally — the precision of a steel ruler with the warmth of oiled wood.

**Theme system**: Users choose their theme at onboarding. Ship with multiple options:
- **Workshop** (default dark): Dark backgrounds with warm accents (amber, terracotta, sage). A workshop at night under warm light.
- **Daylight**: Light cream/sand backgrounds with muted earth accents. A studio with natural light.
- **High contrast**: Full WCAG AAA compliant. Clear, functional, no decorative elements.
- Additional themes can be added over time.

All themes use the same CSS custom property system (`--cp-*` prefix). Runtime switching via `:root` property injection.

**Typography**:
- Primary: **Lexend** — dyslexia-friendly, clean, warm. Already proven in makestack.
- Mono: **JetBrains Mono** — for measurements, data, technical values.
- Display/branding: **Cormorant Garamond** or similar serif — sidebar titles, hero text. Adds craftsmanship feel.

**Anti-references** (explicitly avoid):
- Overly technical / terminal / developer-oriented interfaces (the makestack cyberpunk theme is the anti-reference for CraftPlanner)
- JSON editing, code-like interfaces, or anything that requires technical literacy
- Dense data grids or enterprise dashboard patterns
- Generic AI-generated SaaS aesthetics (cookie-cutter gradients, stock illustrations)

**Positive references**:
- The tactile warmth of a Moleskine notebook or Field Notes
- The clean organization of Notion (structure) without the corporate feel
- Cosplanner's domain understanding (PM model) without its dated web UI
- The "tool-in-hand" feeling of apps like Bear, iA Writer, or Things

### Design Principles

1. **Primitives, not complexity**: Users think in materials, tools, techniques, and projects — not databases, schemas, or relationships. Every technical concept must be abstracted behind a craft metaphor. If you can't explain a feature by pointing at something on a physical workbench, reconsider the feature.

2. **Progressive disclosure**: Show the simple path first. A new project is just a name and a deadline. Custom fields, templates, and linking are there when you reach for them, invisible when you don't. Zero required fields beyond the name.

3. **Connected, not tangled**: The "wheel with spokes" relationship model (primitives linking to primitives) must feel like pinning cards on a corkboard with string — visual, intuitive, undoable. Never like wiring a circuit board.

4. **Every user's workshop is different**: Renamable sidebar items, custom templates, metric/imperial toggle, theme choice. The app adapts to the maker's domain, not the other way around. A cosplayer's "Materials" might be "Fabrics"; a woodworker's "Techniques" might be "Joinery".

5. **Accessible by default**: WCAG AAA as the target. Reduced motion support, high contrast theme, dyslexia-friendly typography (Lexend), large touch targets, keyboard-navigable. Accessibility isn't a checkbox — it's a design constraint that makes the app better for everyone.

### Component Patterns
- **Radix UI primitives** wrapped in custom styled components (existing makestack pattern)
- **TipTap** (ProseMirror) for rich text editing with `[[` autocomplete
- **React Flow** for node/graph visualization
- **Lucide React** for icons — consistent, clean, with optional craft-themed custom icons
- All interactive elements: minimum 44px touch target, visible focus rings, keyboard accessible
